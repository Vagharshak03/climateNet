History of UFAR


The French University in Armenia is a higher education institution established in 2000 between the governments of Armenia and France in 1995. within the framework of the signed cultural, scientific and technical cooperation agreement.

The founders of the university, the RA Ministries of Foreign Affairs and Education and Science, the French Ministry of Foreign Affairs and International Development, and the French Embassy in Armenia, thus emphasized the importance of the education of Armenian youth for the benefit of Armenia and French-Armenian relations. UFAR is a symbol of cooperation between France and Armenia.

Being founded as a joint-stock company, UFAR received the status of an interstate university in 2003 thanks to the 2002-2006 cooperation in RA. Ambassador of France to the efforts of Mr. Henri Cuny.

The vision of UFAR is to become a leader of higher education in the Caucasus and a model French university operating abroad. By providing the necessary tools for the education of impartial, responsible and broad-minded leaders, the university enables young people to become top professionals.
